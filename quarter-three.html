What the viewers are satisfied with

In order to determine what the viewers liked about our stream, we've taken their feedback from a survey form we distributed while the stream was ongoing.

  • Chosen Streaming Topic
  • The viewers are interested in the topic discussed in the first stream.
  • Streamers' Knowledge on the topic
  • The viewers applaud the knowledge of the streamers on the topic.
  • Design of the Content
  • It seems that the viewers enjoy the design used in our streams.
  • Length of the Stream
  • The viewers are satisfied with the length of the first episode.

The team will make sure to take actions appropriate to the feedback received in order to keep the attention of viewers.

What we need to improve on for next time

Despite the viewers’ praises, there are always things we can improve on. These were determined through firsthand experiences from the team members while streaming, along with the criticism and suggestions from respondents of the feedback form.

  • Chosen Streaming Platform
  • The viewers are unsatisfied with the platform the team has chosen to stream on.
  • Audio and Other Equipment
  • The stream eventually had to be rescheduled to the day after due to audio issues.
  • Promotion of the Stream
  • One respondent mentioned that the channel should be promoted more to reach more people.

.

Engagement the stream received

Detailed below is the engagement the team’s first stream has received measured by the YouTube Analytics tool.

  • Audience Retention
  • The stream has an average watch time of 0.4 hours. The average duration of a view is around five minutes and 45 seconds, meaning that the average percentage of the video viewed comes to 9.8%.
  • Concurrent Viewers
  • An average of two viewers were watching the stream live at the same time. The peak number of viewers watching the live stream at the same time was three.
  • Like vs Dislike Ratio
  • Since the publish date, the video has gone on to receive four likes and zero dislikes, totaling to a 100% like-to-dislike ratio.

Stream Viewership

Viewership includes the amount of time viewers have watched your video, total number of views, the number of legitimate views for your channels or video, and the average views per viewer. This data can help the team learn more about its audience and also see how much of an impression the channel and the stream is making on the site.

  • Viewer Profile
  • 97% of channel viewers are subscribed, while the remaining 3% are not.
  • 48% of the viewers are Filipino.
  • Audience Activity
  • The stream managed to attract 4 returning viewers and 13 unique viewers.
  • Impressions Made
  • 27 Impressions were made with a 7.4% success rate at attracting viewers.
  • Channel Traffic
  • Most traffic comes from external sources, with the leading source being Facebook, followed by Messenger and Google Docs.
